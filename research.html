Research

BIA-ALCL is a recently recognised and distinct malignancy of T-lymphocytes exclusively associated with textured breast implants used for both aesthetic and reconstructive surgery. To date, the two most frequently implicated aetiological contributors appear to be the use of textured implants and chronic bacterial stimulation. I have investigated the role of implant textures of varying morphology, the interaction of their surface with bacteria and the potential role of chronic bacterial antigen stimulation in the aetiopathogenesis of this disease.

Our research showed a link between highly textured breast implants, biofilm and anaplastic large-cell lymphoma development and this has resulted in worldwide recognition and withdrawal of selected highly textured breast implants from European markets in 2019: Allergan textured breast implant sales halted in europe amid safety controversy.

Most recently, the Australian TGA acknowledged our research in their decision to ban the use of high-profile textured implants from the Australian market in September 2019: TGA cracks down on all breast implants over cancer fears.
